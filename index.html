Muhammad Ali

Muhammad Ali was a world heavyweight boxing champion who is considered one of the greatest boxers of all time. He was known for his quick wit, his charisma, and his commitment to social justice.

Ali was born Cassius Marcellus Clay Jr. in Louisville, Kentucky, in 1942. He began boxing at the age of 12 and quickly rose through the ranks. In 1964, he defeated Sonny Liston to become the heavyweight champion of the world.

Ali was a controversial figure throughout his career. He refused to serve in the Vietnam War, which led to him being stripped of his title. He was also a vocal opponent of racism and segregation.

Despite the controversy, Ali was a beloved figure around the world. He was known for his sense of humor and his ability to connect with people from all walks of life. He was also a generous philanthropist who donated millions of dollars to charities.

Ali died in 2016 at the age of 74. He is remembered as one of the most iconic figures of the 20th century.
